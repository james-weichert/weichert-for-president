EXPERIENCE

As the student liaison to the “Business in Education” committee for my high school’s school board, I coordinated with students and clubs at the high school level, administrators on the school district level, and businesses on the local level to best combine the ambitions of students with the resources of community stakeholders. From arranging student discounts at nearby cafés to opening doors for students to intern at local businesses, I learned that students reap the most benefit when students work with–and not against–community members.

Furthermore, my work as Senior Class Student Council At-Large Representative at my high school strengthened my leadership and communication skills, especially when representing students in talks with school administrators. While the needs of the residents I represent must remain paramount, it is equally critical to seek to understand the views and limitations of school officials and community members.

My time with student government also gave me experience organizing and managing large events including the homecoming dance, pep rallies, cookouts, hallway decorating events, and more.

Additionally, I gained valuable leadership experience by being involved in the following extracurricular activities:

  • Mock Trial (Captained my team to a state championship)
  • My city’s Young Democrats Association (18-And-Under Representative)
  • Varsity Soccer
  • High School Young Democrats (Vice President)
  • Wind Ensemble (Concertmaster)

I firmly believe that these past experiences have given me a unique perspective on how to best bring opportunities to the residents of Foothill, whether they be fun programming events, community engagement events, or career development opportunities.

COMMUNITY

I believe that as Cal students, we have the unique privilege and responsibility to engage with communities on every level - on campus, in Berkeley, in the U.S., and around the globe. To that end, my goal is to reach beyond the confines of the walls of Foothill to connect with our neighbors in other units and in the wider Berkeley area. It is my hope that by establishing and strengthening partnerships with community members that we can provide numerous service and engagement opportunities to the residents of Foothill.

Of course, we also have our own community within Unit 4. Not only is it important to build bonds with your roommates and suitemates, but it is almost equally important to make friends in another building or on the other side of Hearst Ave. In pursuit of new friendships, I’ll aim to host various socials and community nights including cookouts, movie nights, hikes, and trips into downtown Berkeley. Likewise, we can’t forget our friends over in Stern and WISE (Building 9). Even though our hall associations are separate, our proximity to each other will allow us to pool ideas and resources to the benefit of all residents in Unit 4.

CAREER OPPORTUNITIES

Like every Berkeley student I know, I want to use these next four years to get myself ready to go ‘into the real world’ and get a job that I enjoy. That's why I think it’s important to start exploring possible careers and honing professional skills early on in the college process. As such, I am committed to connecting Foothill residents with professionals in a wide variety of fields. Along with opportunities to network with possible employers, I’ll work to arrange presentations by Berkeley student organizations, resident faculty, and professionals from around the Bay Area.

HOUSING

For the next nine months or so, Foothill is going to be our home, so I want to make this place as homely as possible. From working with our Residence Director to coordinating with the maintenance and cleaning staff, I'll make sure that we as residents of Foothill cultivate positive relationships with the people who help to make our housing experience as enjoyable as possible. Part of the responsibility of cultivating this positive relationship also falls on our backs as residents. It’s our job to clean up after ourselves and to avoid making unnecessary messes, especially in common spaces. After all, the staff are here to support us, not to constantly pick up after us.
